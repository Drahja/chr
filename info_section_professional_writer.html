Doubtless you are either anxious or excited at the very notion of my Professionalism. And for those of you prone to the inundation of self-doubt -- then CONGRATULATIONS -- enjoy the copious combination of BOTH! But before you continue -- I want to make something CLEAR; it's NOT what you think. While many do not understand what it truly MEANS to be a Writer; an OUTSTANDING majority have no understanding of the methods we Writers utilize to complete work. You've likely heard of Drafts; hell, its even more likely you've written several. But there is an even greater possibility that you put too much effort into those Drafts than you should have. Unbeknownst to you; you may already be among many of the countless casualties that have fallen to one of Modern Societies' most nefarious inventions -- Spell Checker. Oh I mean it; this dastardly thing is a plague upon the mind. Ever distracting you with little red lines and suggestions that immediately stifle your unique voice and creativity. Instead of focusing on the story; you become overwhelmed by a sense of ignorance to the craft. Ever intimidated until you're spending more time correcting than you are writing. Hell; I can already guarantee you've fallen into this trap while Roleplaying; especially if you've over-committed to making a Strong Impression to someone above your skill level. I know how easy it is to come to the conclusion that extremely high caliber, clean, and well-executed writing with impeccable punctuation is what I readily produce; or am prone to be smitten by. That is EXACTLY why I'm taking the liberty to tell you that NO; that is absolutely NOT the case. While I retain the capacity for that feat, understand that what we do here does not warrant the methodical care of a College Dissertation. There are only TWO things a First Draft needs to achieve to be considered successful. FIRST; contain MORE material than you will ever use, but the SECOND -- and most important -- is that it just simply needs to be DONE. Editing must be fundamentally ignored if the ending is to EVER be within reach. The First Draft is never destined to be good; committing time to cleaning it -- is as productive as polishing a turd. I've no inclination of ignoring Capital Letters nor Punctuation -- so I'm not going to be shoveling crap in your general direction. As a matter of fact, I’m going to use a dastardly amount of Semi-Colons, Tildes, and Over-Exaggerated Hyperbole. Why? Because I can; that’s why. I’m not here get bitched at by an editor; I’m here to just write some crud at an extremely high output with very fluid pacing. So expect spelling mistakes, typos, and other things. BECAUSE I DO NOT EDIT AT ALL. So for the love of God; please don't treat me like your goddamn English Teacher (fetishes withstanding of course). Relax and take it easy; for only then will you be able to produce something I'll genuinely enjoy.
